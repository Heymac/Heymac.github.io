Youtube Soundsystem

Personal Utility Site

3 Month / Individual Project

Objective

To design a website that allows for more efficient listening and management of Youtube playlists than the official site.

Background

Having grown frustrated with Youtube's clunkiness when it comes to finding, saving, and listening to music playlists, I decided to create my own functional site that would be more suited for music, not videos
I started by making prototypes for the main pages in Figma, and quickly made semi-functional with HTML.
As I decided on more features to include, the styling and layout changed slightly. Overall testing and use by myself and others informed decisions such "where should the queue be?", or "should playlists be docked to the side?".

Final

The site was about 90% functional after a few months of off-on work. As it was being used, feedback was obtained and changes were made over time.
The site works using Youtube's API, which allows interoperability between this site and the official one. Playlists saved here can be viewed on Youtube.com, and vice versa. YS also has features that were, at the time, non-existent on the official site. Queuing, replicating other's playlists, looping tracks, and other features were present which Youtube later added to their platform with the unveiling of Youtube Music, a paid subscription service.
